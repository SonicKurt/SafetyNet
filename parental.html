Parental Controls

  • Parental controls are features that allow parents to restrict the access of content to their children.

    There are two types of parental controls: Content Filters and Usage Controls.

  • Content Filters

    Parents can have filters that limit the content that their children are seeing on the internet. They can block content that could be inappropriate for the children to see depending on their age. If your children on social media sites, you should block any outgoing content. It will prevent sharing any information about your children online.

  • Usage Controls

    Parents can also control the amount of screen time, what apps are installed, and features enabled on your child’s device.

    Many operating systems can track the activity on your device. For instance, Apple has implemented ways on how much time you have been on your device and certain apps as well. Parents can block out certain apps from the App Store or the Goggle Play to be installed onto your child’s device. Video game companies have started to implement settings for parents to turn off the console at a certain time limit or block out any inappropriate video games to play on your video game systems.
